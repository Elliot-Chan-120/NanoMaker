zerobind: protein-specific zero-shot predictor

zero-shot = model predicts properties on a protein it has never seen during training
few-shot = handful of examples (5-50) for a new protein before predicting

most proteins in ChEMBL have few known ligands so the model will constantly face proteins it barely knows
zero-shot performance will tell us whether the model learned chemistry or just memorized the well-studied targets

databases: BindingDB, CHEMBL, PDBbind, DrugBank
output = -log of kinetics (K_i, K_d, IC_50, EC_50)
validated SARS-COV-2 drug-target binding prediction

BindingDB -> public database of Drug-Target Interaction, deposits binding affinity data b/w drugs and target prots

- Y: -log(kinetics) coming from BindingDB and ChEMBL directly

possible concern - most protein targets from BindingDB and ChEMBL are disease-relevant therapeutic targets
- these are things drugs are designed to inhibit / modulate
- big difference though, the binding sites might resemble pathogenic sites, but irl, they could be fused to a
completely different molecule designed for drug delivery or some sort of binding application, not pathogenicity

Protein pocket patch design
- 0.1nM Ki -> exceptionally high-affinity interaction pairs b/w ligand and protein pocket
- significant data downscale, much better for what we have
- high-affinity binding at sub 0.1 nM is almost always driven by precise geometric and electrostatic complementarity
- isolates ligand-pocket pairs that are structurally determined to bind tightly
- drug discovery campaigns routinely use 0.1nM as a threshold for defining a high quality hit worth optimizing
